## 2. How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P.
B = MAP means Behavior happens when Motivation, Ability, and Prompt come together.
- M (Motivation): how much you want to do the habit
- A (Ability): how easy the habit is to do
- P (Prompt): the reminder or trigger
- By increasing ability (making the habit tiny) and using strong prompts, it becomes much easier to start new habits even when motivation is low.

## 5. What is the book's perspective about Identity?
- The book says that the best way to build habits is to focus on who you want to become, not just what you want to achieve. Your habits should match your identity, like “I am a healthy person” instead of “I want to lose weight.”
## 6. Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?
The book explains that habits become easier when you:
- Reduce the steps
- Make the habit simple and accessible
- Remove any friction
When something takes less effort, your brain finds it easier to repeat.
## 7. Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?
To reduce a bad habit, you make it difficult by:
- Adding friction
- Increasing the number of steps
- Making it less convenient
When a habit becomes harder, you naturally stop doing it often.
