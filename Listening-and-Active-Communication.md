## 1. What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening? (Minimum 6 points)
- Focus your attention on the speaker and the topic.
- Keep your personal opinions aside while listening.
- Pay attention to the speaker's body language and facial expressions.
- Use door openers.
- Paraphrase content accurately.
- Ask non-threatening questions.
- Use acknowledgment responses.

## 2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?
According to Fisher’s model, the key points of Reflective Listening are:
- Try to understand the speaker clearly.
- Repeat or restate what the speaker said in your own words to check if you understood correctly.
- Notice the speaker’s feelings, tone, and body language.
- Show that you are listening by giving small responses like “yes”, “okay”, or nodding.
- Ask questions if you need more clarity.
- Make sure both sides agree on the same meaning.

## 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?
- Talking while others are speaking.
- Asking people to repeat many times.
- Interrupting while someone is talking.
- Letting personal feelings affect listening.
- Thinking about conclusions before they finish speaking.
- Not paying attention to body language and facial expressions.

## 4. What can you do to improve your listening?
- Focus your attention on the speaker and the topic.
- Set aside your own feelings and prejudices.
- Pay attention to the speaker's body language and facial expressions.
- Use encouraging phrases to keep the conversation going.
- Ask simple and safe questions.
- Give short replies that show you understand.
